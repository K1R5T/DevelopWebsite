The Purpose of IT in Modern Business

Technology is a big part of life for everyone these days especially with businesses as it makes a big difference it terms of how they run and keep track of data.

Running a business is hard work and there is always an obstacle to overcome in one aspect of the business or another, but having a lot of the business digitised makes things a whole lot easier to keep track of, such as HR being able to manage and sort employees details and wage slips or the company communication system that allows employees and management to keep in touch a lot easier than having to send paper messages to all different floors just to get a 2 word response and waiting a long time for it.

Essentially computers make running a business a little bit easier than it used to be when everything was done manually by hand. Take inventory for example, when a business wants to know how much stock is left of a certain product they can now go on a computer and check the system for accurate figures as all stock is scanned in and out on a server that keeps tally of what’s left, compared to when it was all done by hand having to actually go around doing a stock check at the end of each day counting manually.

Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Some examples of risk in a digital project:
You could be building a website and a few months in, Samsung releases a new software update, and the site you have in development doesn’t work properly on the new system, this can cause a big set back.
Three months into a website build project, a competitor releases a new site with a great new feature that could draw your customers away.
A key team member departs your company for another opportunity mid-way through the project.
Unforeseen circumstances
There are many more risks to a digital project than those listed above, and some things just simply can not be avoided. A project can fail for many reasons, one of those is being over ambitious with the project because expecting perfection instead of embracing the fact that the project is done can result in projects that drag on way past their initially estimated time for completion and can result in the project being failed or incomplete. Finance can also be a big issue and can cause a project to fail, if the budget for a project is not realistic then it has little chance of being successful.

Selecting the right technology for a project depends on the type of project someone is trying to do. During my time on the course we created a text based game, for this we needed a few things to help us in the process. We worked in groups of four and all helped create the game. Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?


GDPR impacts a digital project in terms of how they collect, store and distribute data. When starting a digital project the person or company will have to look at what data they will be collecting, how sensitive the data is and what they will do with that data, in terms of storing data they will need to look at where it is stored, how secure their storage is and how it will be used once collected and stored. They will then need to inform every client or customer in a disclosure explaining all these things so that the customer or client is aware of what is going to happen with their data once it is collected, they will also have to inform the client/customer how long their data will be stored, and what it is used for, as well as informing them of any potential or current data breaches to ensure the data is protected correctly under GDPR regulations.
